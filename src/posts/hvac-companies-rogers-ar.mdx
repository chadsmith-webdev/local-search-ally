## Optimizing Your Google Business Profile for Rogers AR

If you haven't claimed and verified your GBP, start there at business.google.com. Once it's claimed:

**Primary category.** "HVAC Contractor" is the strongest primary category for most HVAC companies. If you primarily do heating, "Heating Contractor." If air conditioning is your main business, "Air Conditioning Contractor." Pick the most accurate one for your actual revenue mix.

**Secondary categories.** Add every relevant one: "Air Conditioning Repair Service," "Furnace Repair Service," "Heating Contractor," "Air Conditioning Contractor," "Heat Pump Supplier," "Mechanical Contractor." Each secondary category expands the searches your profile is eligible to appear for.

**Services.** List and describe every service you offer:
- AC Repair and Service
- Furnace Repair and Service
- Heat Pump Installation and Repair
- HVAC System Replacement
- AC and Heating Tune-Up
- Emergency HVAC Service
- Indoor Air Quality
- Ductwork Repair and Replacement
- Thermostat Installation

Write a 1–3 sentence description for each. Use the city name in at least a few descriptions ("Serving Rogers, Bentonville, and surrounding NWA communities").

**Service area.** Set your service area to include every city where you take jobs: Rogers, Bentonville, Fayetteville, Springdale, Siloam Springs, Centerton, Bella Vista, Cave Springs, Lowell.

**Business description.** Write a 750-character description that covers what you do, how long you've been doing it, your service area, and why someone should call you instead of the next company. No filler. No "we pride ourselves on customer service." Say something true and specific.

**Photos.** Add photos of service vans, your team, equipment installs, and completed work. Add new photos monthly. Google's algorithm rewards consistent activity.

## Building Reviews That Actually Move Rankings

In a competitive HVAC market like Rogers, review velocity matters as much as total count.

The companies leading the Map Pack aren't just sitting on old reviews. They're earning new ones every month. Google's algorithm treats a company with 80 reviews and 8 earned in the last 30 days as more active and trustworthy than one with 150 reviews and none in the last six months.

The most effective system for HVAC companies:

**Automate the ask.** Most HVAC companies use field service management software — ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber, or similar. All of these have review request features that send a text or email after a job closes. Enable it. Set the timing to 2–4 hours after job completion, when the customer still feels the relief of their AC working again.

**Manual follow-up.** For jobs without an automated trigger — commercial accounts, maintenance agreements, large installs — have the technician text the customer directly from their phone after the job: "Hi [Name], glad we could get you taken care of today. If you have two minutes, a Google review would really help our small business. Here's the direct link: [link]."

**Never incentivize.** Don't offer discounts, gift cards, or anything of value in exchange for a review. It's against Google's terms and they detect it.

## Citation Cleanup for HVAC Companies

Citations — online listings with your name, address, and phone — are trust signals for Google. Inconsistent citations create doubt. Consistent ones confirm you're real, local, and stable.

The highest-priority directories for HVAC companies in Rogers AR:

- Google Business Profile
- Yelp
- Facebook Business Page
- Angi
- HomeAdvisor
- Thumbtack
- BBB
- Nextdoor Business
- Bing Places for Business
- Apple Maps
- Houzz (useful for HVAC-adjacent home services)

Check each one. Your business name, address (or service area if you're home-based), and phone number should match exactly — same abbreviations, same formatting, same phone number. A listing that says "Ste 200" when your GBP says "Suite 200" is a minor inconsistency but they add up.

Fix the most important directories first (Google, Yelp, Facebook, Angi), then work down the list.

## The Seasonal Opportunity Most HVAC Companies Miss

HVAC has built-in seasonality. Most NWA homeowners don't think about their AC until the first 90-degree day in May. They don't think about their furnace until the first cold snap in October.

That's the window. If you're ranking in the Map Pack when demand spikes, you capture calls from homeowners who've never heard of you. If you're not ranking yet, you miss the season entirely.

The tactical implication: start your SEO work before the season, not during it.

- Start spring AC prep content and GBP optimization in March.
- Start fall furnace prep content and GBP optimization in August.
- Review generation should run year-round so you're building review velocity heading into each peak season.

A blog post published in March titled "AC Tune-Up Checklist for Rogers AR Homeowners" will be indexed and gaining ranking authority by the time May heat arrives. A post published in June is still building authority when the season ends.
