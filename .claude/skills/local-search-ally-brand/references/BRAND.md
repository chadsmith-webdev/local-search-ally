## Brand Script (StoryBrand SB7)

### 1. Character (The Hero)

NWA home service trade owner who wants more calls from people ready to hire —
without chasing leads or depending on word of mouth alone.

### 2. Problem

**Villain:** Invisibility. You do great work, but online you don't exist.

**External:** Competitors show up in Google's Map Pack. You don't. Customers
searching for your exact service in your exact area never see your name.

**Internal:** It's frustrating — you know the work is good. You just can't
figure out why the other guy gets the calls when you know you do better work.

**Philosophical:** The best contractor in town shouldn't be the hardest one
to find.

### 3. Guide (Local Search Ally)

**Empathy:** "I came to this through real estate wholesaling — spent time in
the contractor world and saw the same problem everywhere. Great work,
completely invisible online."

**Authority:** Third-party data (97% of consumers use Google for local
businesses), demo sites proving the technical method, web development
student closing the SEO-to-site loop.

### 4. Plan

**Process Plan:**
1. **Audit** — Review local visibility, service pages, and competitor presence to find the highest-impact gaps.
2. **Fix Priority Gaps** — Improve what matters first: local relevance, on-page clarity, and lead paths that support calls.
3. **Grow and Track** — Monitor progress and adjust based on what improves visibility and call quality.

**Agreement Plan:**
- No contracts ever
- Radical transparency — you always know what I'm doing and whether it's working
- If I stop delivering, you walk

### 5. Calls to Action

**Direct CTA:** "Let's Talk — It's Free"

**Transitional CTA:** Free SEO Audit Tool (built on the lsa-seo-audit skill) —
earns trust, demonstrates expertise, and opens the door before asking for anything.

**CTA language (always low-pressure and specific):**
- "Let's Talk — It's Free"
- "Start With a Free Conversation"
- "See Where You Stand Online"
- "Get an Honest Assessment"
- "Run Your Free SEO Audit"
- "Book your strategy call →"

**Avoid:** "Buy Now," "Sign Up," "Get Started Today," "Act Fast," anything
that sounds pushy or vague.

### 6. Failure (Stakes of Inaction)

Every month you're invisible is a month your competitor gets those calls.
Referrals don't scale. When word of mouth slows down, there's no system
behind it.

### 7. Success (The Transformation)

Phone rings from people who searched for exactly what you do, in your area.
Schedule fills from online searches, not just referrals. You can see which
searches bring calls and which pages earn work.

## Voice & Tone

**The persona:** Chad at a job site, not Chad in a boardroom. Straight talk,
zero fluff. You're talking to a plumber or roofer — not a CMO.

### Voice Spectrum

| Dimension | Position |
|---|---|
| Formal ↔ Casual | ○○○●○ (casual, but not sloppy) |
| Serious ↔ Playful | ○●○○○ (serious with dry humor) |
| Expert ↔ Approachable | ○○○●○ (approachable first, expertise shows through specifics) |
| Corporate ↔ Rebellious | ○○○●○ (anti-agency, anti-jargon) |
| Reserved ↔ Bold | ○○●○○ (confident center — honest, not loud) |

### 5 Voice Traits

| Trait | What it means in practice |
|---|---|
| **Plain-spoken** | Say the thing directly. No jargon, no filler. If a plumber wouldn't say it at a job site, rewrite it. |
| **Honest** | Say what you know, what you don't, and where you're at. Never claim what isn't proven. |
| **Low-pressure** | Every CTA is a door held open, not a hand on someone's back. The work speaks or it doesn't. |
| **Specific** | Name the trade, the city, the number. Vague = cut. |
| **Personal** | Write as Chad. "I" not "we." One person talking to one person. |

### We Are / We Are Not

| We ARE | We are NOT |
|---|---|
| Direct | Corporate |
| Honest about where we're at | Faking a track record |
| Confident in the method | Promising overnight results |
| A partner | A vendor who disappears |
| Encouraging | Condescending |
| Specific | Buzzword-driven |

### Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Notes |
|---|---|---|

| Banned | Use Instead |
|---|---|
| solutions | results |
| leverage | use |
| synergy | (cut it) |
| cutting-edge | current / proven |

| moves the needle | actually works |

| powered by | built on / using |
| best-in-class | real / proven |
| transform | fix / improve / rank |

| extensive experience | years of hands-on work |
| digital presence | website / GBP / listings |

| drive traffic | get found / rank for |

### 6 Structural Rules

1. **Lead with the problem.** The contractor should see themselves in the first sentence — not your services.
2. **Use "I" — always.** "We" signals an agency trying to sound bigger. That's not Chad.
3. **If you can't name a specific number, city, or trade — the sentence is probably too vague to keep.**
4. **Every CTA should describe the action, not the outcome.** "Let's Talk" beats "Grow Your Business."
5. **Don't oversell size or tenure.** Honesty about being a startup is a feature, not a liability.
6. **If a sentence sounds like it belongs on a stock photo, delete it.** ("Empowering businesses to thrive.")

### Drift Patterns (check every piece of copy)

- **The rule of three** — "more calls, more jobs, more growth." One clear thing said plainly is stronger than three vague things listed together.
- **Agency language sneaking in** — "powered by," "complete solution," "strategic," "digital presence." Name the actual thing.
- **Vague specifics** — claims that gesture at proof without providing it. "Significantly improved visibility" → give a number or cut the claim.
- **Repeated promises** — saying "no pitch / no pressure / honest conversation" three times on the same page dilutes all three. Say it once, well.
- **Double adjective trap** — "good SEO deserves a good website." Reads like a slogan. Name what makes each one good, or cut it.
- **Power word creep** — "dominate," "crush," "own the market." Sounds like a Fiverr gig. Use plain language: "show up first," "rank above," "get into the Map Pack."
- **Hero language** — Positioning yourself as the hero instead of the guide. The contractor is the hero. You help them win.

### Examples — Do This / Not That

**Write like this:**
> "Most NWA contractors do great work — but they're invisible online. I've spent years figuring out how local search actually works. Now I use that to help contractors like you get found and get hired."

**Not like this:**
> "We leverage cutting-edge local SEO strategies and digital marketing solutions to optimize your online presence and drive qualified lead generation."

**Honest confidence:**
> "I'm not a big agency with decades under my belt. I'm a startup founder in Siloam Springs who's obsessed with local SEO, and I've been proving it works on my own projects for years before ever offering it to anyone else."

**Not hollow credibility:**
> "As an industry-leading expert with extensive experience, we deliver best-in-class results for our valued clients."

**Specific CTA:**
> "Get an honest assessment — it's free."

**Not vague CTA:**
> "Start your journey to online success today!"

**Usage rules:**

- All backgrounds are dark — no light themes
- White text always on `--slate` backgrounds
- Carolina text or white text on `--bg` and `--surface` backgrounds
- Steel works as text on dark backgrounds or as a background with white text

**Color combos that work:**
- White `#f0f0f0` text on `#0a0a0a` bg
- Carolina `#7bafd4` text on `#0a0a0a` bg

- Steel `#4A6B8A` text on `#0a0a0a` bg (secondary/muted accent)

## Typography

**Display / Headings:** Fraunces (web) or Georgia (print/PDF fallback), weight 700
**Body / UI:** Urbanist (web) or Helvetica Neue / Arial (print/PDF fallback), weight 400
**Data / Metrics / Technical:** JetBrains Mono (monospace), weight 400–500
**Labels / Eyebrows:** Same sans-serif, weight 600, ALL CAPS, letter-spacing 0.15–0.2em

**When to use JetBrains Mono:**
- Scores and metrics in tools (e.g., "78/100", "STATUS: ACTIVE")
- Audit data, percentages, and status indicators
- Technical labels in dashboards and interactive tools
- Code snippets or terminal-style output
- NOT for marketing copy, proposals, blog posts, or social media

**Scale:**
- Hero/H1: clamp(2.5rem, 6vw, 4.5rem) — letter-spacing −0.02em
- H2: clamp(1.75rem, 3vw, 2.5rem) — letter-spacing −0.02em
- H3: 1.2–1.35rem, weight 500
- Body: 1rem, line-height 1.7
- Small/supporting: 0.875rem
- Eyebrow/caption: 0.65–0.75rem, uppercase, tracking 0.15em
- Data/metric: 0.85–1.1rem in JetBrains Mono, weight 500

**PDF font substitution:** Always use Georgia/Helvetica Neue in HTML that will
be converted to PDF via wkhtmltopdf, since Google Fonts won't load in that context.
JetBrains Mono can be used in PDF only if pre-installed or embedded as base64.

## Founder Story

Chad came to local SEO through real estate wholesaling. Wholesaling runs on
contractor relationships — he spent time in that world and saw the same
problem up close: tradespeople doing great work, completely invisible online.

He's not coming at this with a stack of client case studies. He's a self-taught
developer and SEO student, learning by building real things. What convinced him
local SEO works isn't a portfolio of wins yet — it's the data. Search behavior
for local service businesses is well-documented. The businesses that show up
in the Map Pack capture the overwhelming majority of clicks. Most NWA
contractors aren't there. That gap is the opportunity.

He's learning web development specifically to close the loop — because SEO and
web design aren't two separate services, they're one problem. A site that
doesn't convert wastes the rankings. Rankings without a site waste the traffic.
The goal is to offer both, done right, as one person who understands how they
connect.

The story isn't "I've already done this for 50 contractors." It's "I know why
this works, I know the market, I'm building toward it honestly, and I'll tell
you exactly where I'm at."
