# Local Search Ally — Explainer Video Script

**Duration:** ~75 seconds  
**Format:** 16:9 (website + YouTube)  
**Voice:** Chad speaking direct to camera (Kokoro TTS — male voice)  
**Style:** Problem-first, plain-spoken, no hype

---

## SCENE 1 — THE INVISIBLE JOB LOSS (0:00–0:18)

> Right now, somewhere in Northwest Arkansas, someone's searching "plumber near me" — and they're ready to book. They're not browsing. They're not comparing. They need the job done this week.
>
> And they're calling whoever shows up first.
>
> If that's not you... you just lost a job you didn't even know existed.

**Visual direction:** Dark, moody close-up of a phone screen showing a Google local search. The map pack results glowing. A finger taps the top result. Cut to a phone ringing — but it's someone else's phone.

---

## SCENE 2 — WHY IT HAPPENS (0:18–0:33)

> Here's the thing — it's not because you're bad at what you do. Most contractors I talk to are great at their trade. That's a completely different skill than showing up on Google. And no one taught you that part. It's not a character flaw. It's just a gap.

**Visual direction:** Over-the-shoulder shot of a contractor working skillfully on a job site — hands framing pipes, wiring a panel, installing ductwork. Confident, capable. Then a subtle shift — a laptop open on a truck tailgate showing a barely-there Google listing. The contrast between craftsmanship and digital invisibility.

---

## SCENE 3 — THE FIX (0:33–0:48)

> That's what I do. I'm Chad, and I run Local Search Ally out of Siloam Springs. I do one thing: I help contractors in Northwest Arkansas get found in local search. Not national brands. Not restaurants. Contractors. Your Google profile, your service pages, your website — built to turn searches into calls.

**Visual direction:** Clean, bright workspace. Hands working on a laptop — Google Business Profile open, a well-structured service page visible. Quick cuts showing a GBP listing improving, map pin pulsing, a phone lighting up with an incoming call.

---

## SCENE 4 — THREE STEPS (0:48–1:05)

> Here's how it works — three steps.
>
> First — I audit your current visibility and we talk. I'll show you exactly where you're losing ground and what to prioritize. No mystery.
>
> Second — I build a plan and execute it. Service pages matched to real searches, Google profile dialed in, site structure that makes sense to Google and to the person trying to hire you.
>
> Third — we optimize and grow. I track what's working, cut what's not, and keep pushing your visibility forward.

**Visual direction:** Three distinct visual beats. (1) Split screen — audit report on one side, a video call conversation on the other. (2) Hands building/coding — a service page coming together, GBP edits being made. (3) Analytics dashboard showing upward trends, a contractor's phone buzzing with new calls.

---

## SCENE 5 — THE CLOSE (1:05–1:18)

> If any of that sounds like where you're at — I do a free discovery call. No commitment, no pitch. Just a straight conversation about what's going on with your online presence and whether it makes sense to work together.
>
> Book a call at localsearchally.com. Or don't. But the next time someone searches for what you do — it'd be better if they found you.

**Visual direction:** Chad (or stand-in) speaking calmly, directly. No hard sell energy. Final frame: the Local Search Ally logo on a dark background, the URL localsearchally.com below it, and a subtle glow. Clean. Confident. Done.
