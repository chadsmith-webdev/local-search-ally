Clear Space

Maintain a minimum clear space equal to the height of the logo mark on all four sides. Never crowd the logo with other elements, text, or edge of the page.

Minimum Sizes

Context Minimum size Notes
Favicon 16px Icon mark only — wordmark omitted
Navbar 36px Full lockup with wordmark
App icon (iOS) 180px Apple touch icon — icon on #06080c background
OG image 80px Inside 1200×630 social preview
Print (business card) 24pt / 8mm Minimum for legibility

Logo Don'ts

  • Stretch, skew, or distort the proportions
  • Rotate the logo
  • Change the Carolina Blue ball fill to any other color
  • Apply drop shadows, glows, or bevel effects
  • Place on a busy or low-contrast background
  • Use the wordmark without the icon mark
  • Rasterize below recommended minimum sizes

Usage Rules

  • → All headings use letter-spacing: -0.02em to tighten display at large sizes
  • → Heading line-height: 1.2 · Body line-height: 1.6 · Mono: 1.4
  • → Never mix Exo 2 and Space Grotesk at the same visual level
  • → JetBrains Mono is reserved for labels, hex values, code — not for body text
  • → Use fluid type scales (clamp()) — avoid fixed px sizes on headings

Imagery

Photography Direction

Subject Matter

  • Contractors at work — real job sites, not staged
  • On-location NWA settings (Ozarks, small-town main streets)
  • Technology in context — phone with Google maps, laptop on job site
  • Before/after results — search rankings, review counts

Style & Mood

  • Slightly desaturated — works against the dark UI
  • Edge-lit or natural light, not flat studio light
  • Documentary feel, not advertising polish
  • Authentic people, not stock-photo expressions

Voice & Tone

The Transparent Expert

Our voice is that of a trusted advisor who shares the process, admits the limits, and lets the work speak. Like the contractor who explains what they're doing under your sink. The teacher who makes complex things simple. The founder who builds in public.

Voice Attributes

We Are

  • Direct — say the thing plainly
  • Confident — no hedging on expertise
  • Specific — numbers over vague claims
  • Honest — admit what we don't know
  • Local — rooted in NWA context

We Are Not

  • Jargon-heavy or corporate
  • Arrogant or dismissive
  • Vague or overblown
  • Pushier than necessary
  • Pretending to be national

Sounds Like / Never Sounds Like

Sounds like

  • "Here's exactly what I did and what happened."
  • "I don't know yet — but I'll find out and tell you."
  • "You don't need a long-term contract. If I'm not delivering, leave."
  • "This took three tries to get right. Here's what each try taught me."

Never sounds like

  • "Our proprietary methodology delivers superior outcomes."
  • "You need to trust the process."
  • "As a leading expert in the space…"
  • "Leveraging synergies to maximize your digital footprint."

Tone by Context

Context Tone Example
Marketing / homepage Confident, tangible "Contractors who rank first get the call. We get you to first."
Blog / educational Teaching, transparent "Here's the exact schema markup that passed Google's validator."
Proposals / email Direct, respectful "Based on your map pack position, here's what I'd fix first."
Error / empty states Helpful, unalarming "Nothing here yet — check back after the next index."
Social media Casual, real "Iterated on the logo for two weeks. Here's every version I rejected."

Writing Guidelines

  • Active voice. "We fixed the schema" — not "The schema was fixed."
  • Short sentences. Target 15–20 words for clarity. Break complex ideas into multiple sentences.
  • Specific numbers. "Ranks #3 in Bentonville for HVAC" beats "ranks well locally."
  • Second person. Address the reader directly: "you" and "your business."
  • No jargon without context. Define technical terms the first time, or cut them.

Do's & Don'ts

Do ✓

  • Use approved hex values — no near-matches
  • Maintain logo clear space at all times
  • Use Exo 2 for headings, Space Grotesk for body
  • Export images as AVIF/WebP for web use
  • Write in active, second-person voice
  • Reference specific NWA cities and trades
  • Use fluid type and spacing scales
  • Test all text for WCAG AA contrast

Don't ✕

  • Use off-brand colors as primary elements
  • Stretch, rotate, or recolor the logo
  • Mix Exo 2 and Space Grotesk at the same level
  • Use JetBrains Mono for body text
  • Use low-resolution or oversized uncompressed images
  • Write in passive voice or use corporate jargon
  • Use fixed px font sizes for headings
  • Add drop shadows or glow effects to the logo
